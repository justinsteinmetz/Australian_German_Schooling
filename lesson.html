Different Schools,
Different Rules

Class 8a · Speaking

What's this about?

You're going to talk to a partner about schools — comparing what school is like in Germany with schools in Australia. One person has information about the Australian system. The other knows the German system. Ask questions, describe, and share your opinions.

Role A

You know the German system 🇩🇪

These facts describe the typical German state school system. Your own school may differ — if so, say so. That's interesting too.

  • School starts around 7:45–8:00 and often finishes by early afternoon
  • Students are streamed at around age 10 — Gymnasium, Realschule, or Hauptschule
  • Grades go from 1 (best) to 6 (worst)
  • A lot of written exams; oral exams too, especially at higher levels
  • School uniform is rare — most students wear their own clothes
  • Some schools have a canteen; others don't — families often pay for school materials
  • Around 6 weeks of summer holidays
  • University entrance depends on the Abitur — the final school exam
Role B

You know the Australian system 🇦🇺

Your partner doesn't know much about Australian schools. Explain it using the facts below.

  • School starts around 9:00 and finishes around 3:00–3:30 every day
  • All students follow the same path — no early streaming into different school types
  • Grades use letters (A–E) or percentages, not numbers like 1–5
  • A mix of written exams, projects, and continuous assessment during the year
  • Many schools have uniforms — it's very common, especially in state schools
  • Most schools have a canteen where students can buy lunch
  • Long summer holidays — about 6 weeks in December and January (it's summer there!)
  • University entrance is based on a final score (ATAR) calculated from Year 12 results
1
Warm-up · 5 min
Compare these topics with your partner
School hours

What time does school start and finish? Is there a big difference?

Grades & marks

How are students assessed? Which system seems clearer to you?

Cost & materials

Who pays for textbooks, materials, trips? Is school free in both systems?

Holidays

How long are the holidays? When are they? Is there anything surprising?

Getting into university

How does the system decide who goes to university?

Student voice

Can students choose their subjects? Do they have any say in how their school is run?

Language support
Useful phrases

Describing

  • In our system, students …
  • Here, school usually …
  • In Australia, it's different — …
  • One thing that's similar is …
  • The main difference is …

Comparing

  • That's quite similar to …
  • That's very different from …
  • In contrast, here we …
  • Both systems have …
  • Unlike here, in Australia …

Giving opinions

  • I think that's … (fairer / harder)
  • Personally, I prefer …
  • That sounds … (strange / better)
  • I'm not sure that's a good idea because …
  • What do you think about …?
2
Discussion · 10–15 min
What do you actually think?
Q1

In Germany, students are separated into different school types at around age 10. In Australia, everyone follows the same path until they finish school. Which system do you think is fairer — and who does each system benefit most? (Take your time on this one.)

Q2

Should students wear school uniforms? What are the advantages and disadvantages? Would you want a uniform at your school?

Q3

In Australia, there's a mix of continuous assessment and final exams. In Germany, the final exam carries a lot of weight. Which approach do you think is more accurate — does one test really show what you know?

Q4

Is it fair that getting into university depends almost entirely on final exams? What else should count?

Q5

One thing you would change about your own school system — what is it, and why?

Listen for …

  • Comparative structures (more … than, -er than)
  • Opinion phrases (I think, personally …)
  • Contrast connectors (but, however, unlike)

Push further if …

  • Partner only gives one-word answers → ask "Why do you think that?"
  • You agree on everything → try to find one thing to disagree about

Timing

Wrap-up · 5 min · Whole class
Report back

Your task

Each pair chooses one thing from your conversation to share with the class. It can be something surprising, something you disagreed about, or something you think is important. You have about 30 seconds each.

Option A — A surprise

"We were surprised to find out that …"

Option B — A disagreement

"We didn't agree about … I think … but my partner thinks …"

Option C — A strong opinion

"We both think that … because …"

Option D — A question

"Something we couldn't answer was … Does anyone know?"

Starting your report

  • We found out that …
  • We were surprised that …
  • We talked about … and we think …
  • One thing we noticed was …

Reporting a disagreement

  • We didn't agree about …
  • I thought … but my partner thought …
  • We had different opinions on …
  • In the end, we couldn't decide …

Responding to other pairs

  • We thought the same thing.
  • We had a different opinion — we think …
  • That's interesting because we …
  • Can you say more about …?
